Annual Events in St. George

From world championship triathlons to the largest senior games on the planet, St. George hosts events that draw athletes, spectators, and visitors from around the globe year after year.

The Big Three

These world-class events put St. George on the international map. Thousands travel here specifically to participate.

🏅 Huntsman World Senior Games — October

The world's largest annual multi-sport event for athletes 50 and over. Over 30 sports — from track & field to pickleball, swimming to cycling. More than 11,000 participants from 80+ countries have competed since the games began. The entire city gets involved, with events held at venues all across St. George. Opening ceremonies at Greater Zion Stadium are a celebration of active aging and community spirit.

🏃 St. George Marathon — October

Consistently ranked among the top marathons in the United States. This point-to-point course starts in Pine Valley at 5,200 feet and drops 2,600 feet through scenic red rock canyons before finishing in downtown St. George. Known for its fast, net-downhill course, it's a popular Boston qualifier. The race has been running for over 40 years and draws more than 7,000 runners annually.

stgeorgemarathon.com →

🚴 IRONMAN 70.3 North American Championship — Spring/Fall

St. George has hosted the IRONMAN 70.3 World Championship (2021-2022) and continues as the North American Championship. The course features a challenging bike through Snow Canyon State Park and a run through the red rock desert — considered one of the toughest yet most scenic courses on the circuit. Hundreds of professional and age-group triathletes compete for world championship slots.

Event Planning Tips

  • Book early: During Ironman, the Marathon, and Senior Games, hotels fill up months in advance. Reserve 6-12 months ahead for peak events.
  • October is the busiest month: The Marathon and Senior Games often overlap or run back-to-back. The city is vibrant but packed — plan accordingly.
  • Weather varies: October averages 70-80°F for race day, but mornings can be 40°F. Spring events can bring wind. Always pack layers.
  • Spectator-friendly: All three major events have excellent spectator viewing points. Course maps are published in advance.
  • Community feel: St. George takes tremendous pride in these events. Locals line the streets, volunteer in droves, and welcome visitors like old friends.
